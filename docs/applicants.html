Information for Applicants

Linguistics graduate program

If your interest is in the Linguistics doctoral program, I am always happy to supervise admitted students whose interests align with my own. However, I do not have a lab per se, nor do individual faculty members make admissions decisions. In our department, the faculty as a whole evaluates admission applications by consensus. For a candidate to be successful, a majority of the faculty must be in favor. You are of course very welcome to apply to the program; there is information on the department web page.

In their statements of purpose, candidates frequently mention faculty members that they would like to work with, and you are welcome to do so. However, it is not the practice of faculty members, including myself, to endorse candidates in advance.

Postdocs and visiting professors

There are currently no postdoc or visiting-professor positions that I am involved in. In the absence of any advertised positions, I am unable to provide any assistance to those seeking such positions.

Computer Science

If your interest is in the Computer Science department, I'm afraid I cannot help you. I do not have a position in Computer Science, and though I am an affiliate, being an affiliate does not authorize me to supervise Computer Science students. For that reason, and given the large volume of requests, inquiries about the Computer Science department will not receive a response.
